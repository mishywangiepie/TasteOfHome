BEEF NOODLE SOUP

Introduction

A home favorite, you can get variations of this bowl for less than 3 dollars in Taiwan. Though this dish requires some preparation with the meat braising, you can make large batches of the meat and freeze it and the jus for very quick prep later on. The quickest, most satisfying meals of my college life consist of frozen meat my mom sends to me.

Prep

  • Rice noodles, thin or thick
  • Beef shank, with a good mix of tendon and fat
  • Bok Choy, or similar meaty veggies
  • Anise and other spices
  • Herb garnish

Braise the meat in large chunks in soy sauce, broth, and spices until tender. This step may take multiple hours. When done, thinly slice the meat and reserve the braising liquid.

Cook

Boil water in a large pot and add asian wheat noodles when the water is boiling, adding cold water if the noodles boil over. Add sesame oil if desired, this will help keep the noodles from sticking. When the noodles are almost done, toss in the bok choy or other veggies to flash boil, drain and set aside. Use the braising liquid and meat as soup base, adding the noodle water to dilute to the desired flavor, adding salt to taste.

Serve

Serve the noodles with the veggies with heaping spoonfuls of broth and large chunks of meat. This can get a little messy with noodle slurping, so don't forget the napkins!
