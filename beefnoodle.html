PRESERVED EGG CONGEE

Introduction

This is one of my favorite cold day foods. Originally a breakfast dish served at dim sum with fresh wonton crisps and green onion as garnish, this is the perfect food to cuddle up in bed with. Although some people are averse to preserved egg, or "thousand year old egg", it is a delicacy and one of my favorite foods.

Prep

  • Rice, cooked or uncooked
  • Water and/or broth
  • Lean meat
  • Preserved Egg
  • Veggies
  • Herb garnish

Slice the meat and egg, finely chopping the veggies that will be in the congee, leave aside chopped garnish like cilantro, green onion, or fried wonton crisps aside.

Cook

Cover the rice with 1-2 inches of water and/or broth and boil until the rice swells and begins to lose its structure. Add salt, white pepper, soy sauce to taste. This might take up to an hour for uncooked rice, or 20 minutes for cooked rice. Add the meat and congee veggies at the last 20 minutes, almost immediately for cooked rice. Continue to taste and season with salt, white pepper, and soy sauce.

Serve

Remove from heat when consistency is as desired. Top with the garnish veggies and crisps if prepared. Snuggle up with your piping hot bowl of congee and enjoy! Be careful not to burn your mouth.
